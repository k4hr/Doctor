Decision 1: Doctor Incentive Strategy
Lever ID: 0da041f6-cd6a-49cb-98dd-cfce72e22e63

The Core Decision: The Doctor Incentive Strategy defines how doctors are compensated for their participation on the platform. It controls the commission structure, potential equity/revenue sharing, and performance-based rewards. The objective is to attract and retain high-quality doctors, motivating them to provide timely and accurate answers. Key success metrics include doctor acquisition rate, doctor retention rate, average response time, and doctor satisfaction scores. A well-designed strategy ensures a sustainable and profitable ecosystem.

Why It Matters: Immediate: Increased doctor participation → Systemic: Higher quality answers and faster response times → Strategic: Enhanced user satisfaction and platform reputation, leading to organic growth. Trade-off: Doctor profitability vs. platform revenue.

Strategic Choices:

Standard Commission Model: Offer a fixed commission split (e.g., 80/20) on all consultations and questions.
Tiered Commission Model: Implement a tiered commission structure based on doctor performance metrics (e.g., answer quality, response time, user ratings).
Equity & Revenue Share Model: Offer top-performing doctors equity or a revenue share in addition to commission, fostering long-term commitment and platform ownership.
Trade-Off / Risk: Controls Doctor Acquisition vs. Platform Profitability. Weakness: The options don't address the initial bootstrapping problem of attracting doctors before significant user demand exists.

Strategic Connections:

Synergy: This lever strongly synergizes with the Content Quality Control Strategy. Higher incentives can motivate doctors to provide higher-quality answers, which then reduces the moderation burden. It also supports the User Acquisition Strategy by attracting more doctors, which increases the platform's appeal to users.

Conflict: The Doctor Incentive Strategy can conflict with the Pricing Model Strategy. Higher doctor incentives may necessitate higher prices for users, potentially impacting user acquisition and retention. It also constrains the platform's profitability if incentives are too generous.

Justification: Critical, Critical because it directly impacts doctor acquisition, retention, and quality, influencing both user satisfaction and platform profitability. Its synergy and conflict texts highlight its central role in balancing doctor earnings and platform revenue.
